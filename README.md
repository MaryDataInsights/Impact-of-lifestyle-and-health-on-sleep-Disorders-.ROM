This project analyzes how lifestyle factors (stress, occupation, activity level) and health indicators (BMI, gender) affect the prevalence of sleep disorders. Using a structured dataset, the analysis reveals that stress and high-responsibility professions are the main drivers of sleep disorders, while BMI plays a secondary role. The findings highlight the multifactorial nature of sleep disorders and support recommendations such as workplace stress management, sleep hygiene education, and promotion of healthy lifestyles to reduce prevalence and improve public health outcomes.

Observations

The analysis demonstrates that sleep disorders are driven by a complex interaction of lifestyle and health factors, rather than a single dominant variable. Stress emerges as the most influential factor, with high-stress professions consistently showing higher disorder prevalence. Occupation further reinforces this pattern, indicating that environmental and structural work conditions significantly affect sleep health.

Interestingly, BMI does not serve as a primary predictor in this dataset, as individuals with normal BMI still exhibit high rates of sleep disorders. This challenges traditional assumptions and shifts the focus toward psychological and occupational factors.

Overall, the findings suggest that sleep disorders are multifactorial, requiring a holistic approach to understanding and intervention.

Recommendations

Based on the analysis, the following recommendations are proposed:

Workplace Stress Management Programs

Organizations, especially in high-stress professions, should implement stress reduction initiatives such as counseling, flexible schedules, and mental health support.

Awareness Campaigns on Sleep Hygiene

Public health bodies should educate individuals on the importance of sleep, including practices such as maintaining consistent sleep schedules and reducing screen time.

Regular Health Monitoring

Routine screening for sleep disorders should be encouraged, even for individuals with normal BMI.

Promotion of Work-Life Balance

Policies that limit excessive working hours can significantly reduce stress-related sleep disorders.

Encouraging Physical Activity

Regular exercise should be promoted as it improves sleep quality and reduces stress.
